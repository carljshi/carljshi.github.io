Jingcheng (Carl) Shi

Hi, I'm Jingcheng (Carl) Shi


I'm currently a Ph.D. candidate in Neurobiology and Behavior at Columbia University, advised by Attila Losonczy.


My research focuses on understanding the molecular profiles of functionally distinct neurons in mouse hippocampus


Before graduate school, I was a technician/lab manager at University of California, San Francisco, working with Vikaas Sohal. I studied how gamma synchrony between prefrontal PV interneurons affect information processing and cognitive flexibility.


And before that, I received my B.A. in Molecular and Cell Biology from University of California, Berkeley. I did my undergraduate research in the lab of Dan Feldman.
